Step 1 of 4

Welcome to VP Chef Relay

This app runs on your PC and connects your IP cameras to the VP Chef Studio mobile app. Let's get your cameras configured — it only takes two minutes.

What this app does:
Runs in your system tray, reads live video from your IP cameras over your local WiFi network, and streams footage to the VP Chef Studio cloud so you can control recordings from your phone.
Requirements:
• Node.js installed on this PC
• IP cameras on the same WiFi network
• FFmpeg installed (or placed in the relay folder)
Step 2 of 4

Configure Your Cameras

Enter the local IP address for each camera. You can find the IP in your router's device list or the camera's own app. Click Test to verify each one is reachable.

Cameras that can't be reached will still be saved — they'll show as offline in the dashboard until they come online.
Step 3 of 4

Camera Credentials

Most IP cameras require a username and password to access the video stream (RTSP). Enter the credentials set in your camera's admin panel. The default is often admin / admin or blank.

These credentials are stored locally in relay-config.json next to the relay program — they are never sent to the cloud.
Step 4 of 4

Ready to Go!

Your configuration has been saved. Click Start Relay to launch the camera relay. It will run in your system tray and connect automatically to your cameras.

✓ Cameras configured
Next step — mobile app:
Open VP Chef Studio on your phone, go to Settings → Camera Setup, and enter this PC's IP address (192.168.x.x) to complete the connection.
The relay starts automatically when you open this app in the future. You can control it from the system tray icon at any time.
